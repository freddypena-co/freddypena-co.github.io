My approach

My proposal lies in the intersection between the technology, customer needs, product and the mental health of the people that builds them.

People first. Cultivate trust

Behind great products there's always great people. As in sports, matches are won by teams that excel at collaborating and each individual knows exactly their role, strengths and opportunities, and those of their teammates. Where communications flows transparently, problems are shared and people are treated like adults, especially when things aren't going great. Where trust and certainty prevail, mistakes are treated as learning opportunities and it's ok to have good and bad days.

Engineering and art are means towards an end

The best technology is the one that solves problems inherent to your organization, taking into consideration the expertise and maturity of the teams. The best artistic direction is the one that your artists feel excitement with, true to their expression. The best UI/UX is the one that customers understand.

Team autonomy is proportional to identity and morale

In distributed teams, a federated structure where each team behaves as a micro company is, from my own experience, the key to success. An organization is fundamentally a group of people making decisions small and big on a daily basis. You've hired competent people, so give them the opportunity to shine and, of course, fail, providing them with proactive coaching that creates more leaders â€” and less bosses â€” that make decisions and manage their own priorities, progressing towards the "Performing" stage of team development in accordance to Tuckman's model..

Motivation and productivity are best achieved by establishing attainable goals in consensus

Productivity is directly proportional to individual and group motivation, which depends on the establishment and tracking of clear, tangible and attainable goals in consensus and in the right direction dictated by the strategy devised by leadership. A support system with clear separation of responsibilities, where experts and juniors collaborate, is as fundamental as regularly celebrating their achievements!

Adaptation is another word for "Resiliency"

Trends. Customer needs. Technologies. Macroeconomy environment. The team. The only constant is change. Even when things are going well and you've achieved status quo, the best strategy is to keep evolving. Few changes will make you obsolete, while too many induce anxiety. How to find balance? The key lies in constant (over)communication, solid decision-making with clear accountability, and a good reasoning of "why?".
